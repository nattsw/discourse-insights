Why did traffic spike on Thursday?
Thursday's traffic was 2.3x your daily average, driven almost entirely by a post on r/webdev titled "Best community-driven API docs I've seen" which linked to your Storage API guide. It received 340 clicks from Reddit alone. The spike brought 82 first-time visitors, 14 of whom created accounts. Traffic from this source is tapering but still above baseline.
Which categories need attention?
General Discussion is your weakest category right now — replies are down 5%, unique contributors dropped 12%, and it's been declining for 3 consecutive weeks. Consider pinning a discussion prompt or highlighting recent topics.

Feature Requests is surging (+20%) but has the lowest response rate from staff (23%). Users may feel unheard. Bug Reports is healthy with strong community participation.
What content should I create next?
Based on search data, your biggest content gaps are:

1. "dark mode" — 45 searches, only 12% CTR. Users are looking for this and not finding it. A guide or FAQ would likely perform well.
2. "pricing" — 22 searches, 8% CTR. This suggests users can't find pricing information easily.
3. "export data" — 15 searches, 33% CTR. Partial content exists but could be improved.

Your Storage API guide and Webhook best practices are performing well — more content in that style (practical how-to guides) resonates with your audience.
How is support deflection trending?
Support deflection is trending up. This week: 89 topics solved by the community (68% solve rate, up from 61% last month). Average first-response time improved to 2.4h from 3.1h.

At an estimated $15/ticket, your community deflected roughly $1,335 this week or $5,700/month at current pace. The top community responders are Dev_Guru (42 posts), ReactFan (38), and NoobMaster (25).
